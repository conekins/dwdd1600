History of Video Games

It all began in the 1950s and 1960s when scientists and engineers experimented with analog and digital computers to create simple interactive games. The 1970s marked the birth of the arcade game industry, with the release of "Pong" by Atari in 1971, a simplified version of table tennis that became a massive success. This success led to the development of home video game consoles in the late 1970s, with the Magnavox Odyssey in 1972 and the Atari 2600 in 1977, popularizing gaming in living rooms.


After that there was a time where video games were not being recieved well due to lack of quality and overall entertainment of the games. Nintendo, a now popular video game company, was the one who breathed new life into video games with classic arcade games like Donkey Kong, Super Mario Bros, The Legend of Zelda, and Metroid to name a few.


Nintendo had brought video games out of the rut that they were in and had launched them into the phenomenon that they are today.
